## 10 Essential AI Prompts Every Educator Needs

### 1. The Lesson Plan Generator

```
Create a detailed lesson plan for:

Subject: [e.g., 5th Grade Science]
Topic: [e.g., Water Cycle]
Duration: [40 minutes]
Learning Objectives: [List 3-4 goals]

Include:
- Hook activity (5 min)
- Main instruction (20 min)
- Interactive exercise (10 min)
- Assessment method (5 min)

Adapt for: [visual/kinesthetic/auditory learners]
```

**What it solves:** Eliminates 2-3 hours of planning per lesson

---

### 2. The Differentiation Engine

```
Design a tiered assignment for:

Topic: [Photosynthesis]
Student levels:
- Struggling (30% of class)
- On-grade (50% of class)  
- Advanced (20% of class)

Each tier should:
- Take roughly the same time
- Address the same core concept
- Build appropriate challenge

Include success criteria for each level.
```

**What it solves:** Every student works at their edge, not above or below

---

### 3. The Engagement Architect

```
Create 3 classroom activities for:

Concept: [Fractions]
Age group: [8-9 years old]
Class size: [25 students]
Duration: [10 minutes each]

Requirements:
- Whole-class participation
- Minimal materials needed
- Clear connection to learning goal
- High energy / low prep

Include: Rules, variations, and potential pitfalls
```

**What it solves:** Dead air and disengagement

---

### 4. The Assessment Designer

```
Build a formative assessment for:

Unit: [American Revolution]
Format: [Quiz / Project / Discussion]
Purpose: [Check understanding before final exam]

Include:
- 10 questions spanning Bloom's Taxonomy
- Rubric for evaluation
- Common misconceptions to watch for
- Follow-up questions for students who struggle
```

**What it solves:** Guesswork about what students actually understand

---

### 5. The Parent Communication Specialist

```
Draft a message to parents about:

Situation: [Student's grade dropped from B to D]
Student context: [Bright but distracted, struggling with homework completion]
Parent temperament: [Supportive but concerned]

Tone: Professional, empathetic, solution-focused

Include:
- Acknowledgment of concern
- Specific observations (with data)
- Concrete action plan
- How parents can help at home
```

**What it solves:** Awkward conversations and unclear expectations

---

### 6. The Feedback Personalizer

```
Generate individualized feedback for student essays on:

Topic: [Persuasive writing - Should schools have uniforms?]
Grade level: [7th grade]

For each student, analyze:
- Thesis strength
- Evidence quality  
- Organization
- Voice and style

Provide:
- 2 specific strengths
- 2 actionable improvements
- 1 stretch goal for next assignment

Keep feedback encouraging but honest.
```

**What it solves:** Generic comments that students ignore

---

### 7. The Curriculum Mapper

```
Create a 6-week unit plan for:

Subject: [Biology]
Topic: [Cell Structure and Function]
Meets standards: [List relevant standards]

Break down into:
- Weekly themes
- Daily learning targets
- Formative assessments (weekly)
- Summative assessment (end of unit)
- Required materials and resources

Ensure progressive complexity and integration.
```

**What it solves:** Disjointed lessons that don't build on each other

---

### 8. The Behavior Intervention Guide

```
Design a behavior support plan for:

Student profile: [Calls out in class, disrupts transitions]
Age: [10 years old]
Suspected cause: [Attention-seeking / boredom]

Provide:
- Proactive strategies (prevent the behavior)
- In-the-moment responses
- Positive reinforcement system
- Data tracking method
- When to escalate to administration
```

**What it solves:** Reactive discipline that doesn't change patterns

---

### 9. The Professional Development Synthesizer

```
Summarize this education research paper:

[Paste article or provide topic: "Growth mindset interventions"]

Extract:
- Core findings (3-5 bullet points)
- Classroom applications (practical steps)
- Potential limitations
- 2-3 discussion questions for staff meeting

Translate academic language into teacher-friendly terms.
```

**What it solves:** Dense research that never makes it into practice

---

### 10. The Resource Curator

```
Find and organize resources for:

Topic: [Teaching empathy to middle schoolers]
Formats needed: [Videos, articles, lesson plans, discussion prompts]
Time available: [3 class periods]

Prioritize:
- Free and accessible  
- Evidence-based approaches
- Age-appropriate
- Culturally responsive

Provide brief annotations for each resource.
```

**What it solves:** Hours lost to Google rabbit holes

## Why Most Teachers Don't Use AI Yet

Three myths persist:

**Myth 1:** "It's cheating"  
**Reality:** Using AI for planning is like using a calculator for math — it's a tool, not a shortcut

**Myth 2:** "It will replace me"  
**Reality:** AI can't build relationships, read a room, or adapt in real-time

**Myth 3:** "I don't have time to learn it"  
**Reality:** 30 minutes learning prompts saves 10+ hours per week
